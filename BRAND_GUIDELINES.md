# TLTD STUDIOS BRAND GUIDELINES

## Brand Positioning
TLTD Studios is not simply a design agency. It is becoming a business operating system for ambitious brands, builders, creators, and growth-focused companies.

The brand must communicate:
- Innovation
- Precision
- Intelligence
- Technology
- Architecture
- Design
- Business growth
- Connected systems
- Premium quality
- African innovation with global standards

## Brand Promise
TLTD Studios creates premium systems for businesses, brands, spaces, products, and opportunities. Every experience should feel like part of one unified ecosystem.

## Brand Personality
- Premium
- Minimal
- Editorial
- Futuristic
- Intelligent
- Confident
- Expensive-looking
- Calm but ambitious

## Core Color Palette
- Primary Black: #0B0B0B
- Secondary Black: #1A1A1A
- Primary White: #FFFFFF
- Light Gray: #F5F5F3
- Medium Gray: #DADADA
- Primary Orange: #F25A1C
- Accent Orange: #FF6A00

## Color Usage Rules
- Use black as the foundation of professionalism and structure.
- Use white for clarity, space, and precision.
- Use orange sparingly to guide attention and signal creativity, opportunity, and momentum.
- Orange should never overpower the composition.
- Light gray should be used for soft surfaces, backgrounds, and elevated neutrals.
- Dark black and charcoal tones should anchor typography, shapes, and structure.

## Visual Language
The visual language should feel like a fusion of Apple, Stripe, Linear, Framer, Notion, Figma, Awwwards, Pentagram, Google Material 3, and Adobe MAX without copying any of them.

The system should feel:
- Clean
- Premium
- Technological
- Architectural
- Editorial
- Interactive
- Product-led
- Emotionally intelligent

## Illustration Direction
All illustrations must feel like part of one unified TLTD system.

### Principles
- Use premium 3D objects and semi-isometric compositions.
- Build layered depth with foreground, middle ground, background, and floating elements.
- Use soft ambient shadows, rounded forms, glass-like surfaces, matte materials, and subtle reflections.
- Prioritize clarity, hierarchy, balance, and story over decoration.
- Avoid generic stock-style imagery and avoid business people as the main subject.

### What illustrations should communicate
Illustrations should explain the concept visually before the user reads the text.

Examples:
- Brand strategy should show business cards, typography sheets, packaging, color palettes, and brand books.
- Architecture should show blueprints, building models, steel structures, CAD screens, and construction elements.
- Engineering should show technical drawings, structural forms, measurement tools, and drafting systems.
- Interior design should show furniture, lighting, materials, rooms, and mood boards.
- Web design should show laptops, phones, browser windows, UI mockups, and dashboards.
- Software should show code editors, API flow, cloud systems, database visuals, and automation logic.
- Content creation should show cameras, microphones, drones, editing timelines, lighting, and studio equipment.
- Marketing should show dashboards, analytics, campaigns, funnels, growth arrows, and content systems.
- Business ecosystem should show connected nodes flowing between business, branding, marketplace, projects, funding, education, and growth.

## Icon Direction
Do not use generic icon packs.
Create custom icons that share one visual language:
- Rounded
- Minimal
- Premium
- Consistent
- Designed to feel native to the TLTD system

## Composition Rules
Every illustration should tell a story.
- Arrange elements to guide the eye naturally.
- Create hierarchy through scale, spacing, and contrast.
- Leave intentional negative space.
- Use movement through floating elements, flow lines, and soft perspective.

## Lighting and Material Rules
- Use soft studio lighting.
- Use natural reflections and subtle highlights.
- Avoid harsh lighting and overly glossy plastic.
- Keep materials feeling tactile, polished, and refined.

## Motion and Interaction Thinking
Design illustrations as if they will later animate.
- Floating objects
- Rotating elements
- Expanding cards
- Flowing connection lines
- Subtle glow and hover-ready motion
- Interactive dashboard and system-like behavior

## Logo Usage
- Use the official TLTD logo on clean, uncluttered backgrounds.
- Preserve correct aspect ratio and spacing.
- Do not distort, recolor, or add unnecessary effects.

## Clear Space
Maintain generous clear space around the logo, equal to the height of the logo mark.

## Typography Direction
Typography should feel bold, modern, and editorial.
- Use large headlines with strong hierarchy.
- Keep copy minimal and confident.
- Let visuals carry meaning where possible.

## Writing Style
- Short, precise, confident sentences
- Premium but approachable
- Intelligent and strategic
- Avoid generic corporate language

## Final Standard
Every page, section, and illustration should feel like it belongs to the same premium TLTD product experience.
The result should feel like an Awwwards-worthy digital product — not a standard business website.
